1. What cookies are

Cookies are small text files or similar storage technologies placed on a device when a website is used. UK rules require clear information about their purposes and, unless an exemption applies, consent before they are used.

2. Cookies Ownerlane currently uses

Ownerlane currently uses only first-party cookies that are strictly necessary to deliver requested features, authenticate users and protect the service. We do not currently use advertising or analytics cookies.

CookiePurposeTypical duration
sessionMaintains the signed-in session and security data.Normally until the browser session ends, subject to application settings.
remember_tokenKeeps a user signed in on the device after the browser closes.Up to approximately 12 months unless removed or the user logs out.

These cookies are essential to services requested by the user. A consent banner is therefore not currently displayed. If Ownerlane introduces optional analytics, advertising or other non-essential technologies, they will be blocked until the required choice has been obtained and this notice will be updated.

3. Third-party services

When you choose a paid plan, you may be redirected to Stripe-hosted checkout or billing pages. Stripe may set cookies on its own domains for fraud prevention, security and payment functionality under Stripe’s privacy notice and cookie information.

The site also retrieves presentation resources from content-delivery and font providers. Those network requests may expose ordinary technical information such as an IP address and browser details to the provider, but Ownerlane does not use those resources to place analytics or advertising cookies.

4. Your controls

You can block or delete cookies through your browser. Blocking essential cookies may stop login and security features from working. Logging out removes the active authenticated session, although browser-stored cookies may remain until they expire or are deleted.
