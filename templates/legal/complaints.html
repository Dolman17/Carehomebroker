1. How to complain

Email {{ legal_contact_email }} with “Formal complaint” in the subject line. Include:

  • your name, organisation and account email;
  • the listing, introduction, payment or service concerned;
  • what happened and when;
  • supporting documents or screenshots;
  • the outcome you are seeking.

Please avoid sending unnecessary personal data or confidential transaction documents by ordinary email. We can arrange a safer transfer method where needed.

2. What happens next

  1. We aim to acknowledge the complaint within five working days.
  2. A person with appropriate independence from the subject of the complaint will review the available records and may request more information.
  3. We aim to issue a written outcome within 20 working days. Complex matters may take longer; if so, we will explain why and provide an update.
  4. If you remain dissatisfied, you may request one internal review by responding within 14 days and identifying the points you believe were missed.

3. Urgent confidentiality or security concerns

Mark the email “Urgent confidentiality incident” where restricted listing information, credentials or documents may have been exposed. Change your password immediately if account access may be compromised.

4. Data protection complaints

Privacy concerns can be raised under this procedure or sent directly to the privacy contact above. You also have the right to complain to the Information Commissioner’s Office.

5. Professional advisers and transactions

An adviser listed on Ownerlane remains responsible for its own professional services and complaints process. Disputes between a buyer and seller are generally matters for those parties and their advisers, although Ownerlane may review marketplace conduct or facilitate communication. Contractual dispute rights and limitation periods are not suspended while a complaint is investigated.
