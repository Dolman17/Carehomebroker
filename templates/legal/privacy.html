2. Personal data we collect

  • Account data, including your email address, encrypted password, role and account status.
  • Buyer, seller and adviser profile data, including names, businesses, telephone numbers, professional details, acquisition criteria and transaction preferences.
  • Buyer qualification data, including identity and business details, declared acquisition history, private proof-of-funds evidence and review outcomes.
  • Listing and transaction data, including business information, financial ranges, enquiries, shortlists, valuation requests, introductions, offers and deal progress.
  • Documents and media that you choose to upload, including listing photographs, seller documents and heads of terms.
  • Subscription and payment metadata, such as your Stripe customer, subscription and payment identifiers. Ownerlane does not store full payment-card details.
  • Communications, support requests, complaints and records of email notifications.
  • Technical, security and usage data generated when the service is used, such as session information, server logs and one-way visitor identifiers used to aggregate listing views and shortlist activity.

4. Who receives personal data

Information may be shared only as needed with:

  • buyers, sellers, valuers and advisers involved in an approved marketplace workflow;
  • hosting, database, email, security and technical service providers acting for us;
  • Stripe for checkout, subscription and billing services;
  • professional advisers, insurers, auditors and prospective business purchasers;
  • courts, regulators, law-enforcement bodies or other parties where disclosure is legally required.

Public and basic-access listings are designed to conceal the business identity and restricted commercial information. Disclosure at later stages remains subject to access controls, approvals and any applicable NDA.

Private proof-of-funds files are available only to the submitting buyer and authorised Ownerlane administrators. Sellers may see verification outcomes and buyer-supplied track-record information, but not the underlying evidence file.

Seller analytics are aggregate. Sellers can see activity totals, conversion and buyer-quality summaries, but not the identity of an individual listing visitor. Ownerlane does not use an analytics or advertising cookie for this reporting.

5. International transfers

Some suppliers may process data outside the United Kingdom. Where this happens, we use an available legal transfer mechanism and appropriate safeguards, such as UK adequacy regulations or approved contractual protections. Contact us for information about safeguards relevant to your data.

6. Retention

We retain data only for as long as reasonably needed for the purposes above, including providing an active account, managing a transaction, meeting tax and accounting obligations, resolving disputes and establishing legal claims. Retention depends on the record: active marketplace records may be kept for the life of the account or transaction, while contractual and payment records may need to be retained for longer statutory periods.

You may ask us for the retention criteria applying to a particular record.

7. Your rights

Depending on the circumstances, UK data protection law may give you rights to:

  • access your personal data and receive a copy;
  • correct inaccurate or incomplete data;
  • request erasure or restriction;
  • object to processing based on legitimate interests or to direct marketing;
  • receive certain data in a portable format;
  • withdraw consent without affecting earlier lawful processing;
  • challenge a decision based solely on automated processing where applicable.

Ownerlane’s matching tools support recommendations; they do not make solely automated decisions producing legal or similarly significant effects.

8. Security and confidentiality

We use role-based access, subscription controls, signed sessions, password hashing, CSRF protection and private document storage. No online service is completely risk-free, so users must also protect their credentials and disclose confidential information cautiously.

9. Complaints and changes

Please contact us first so we can investigate. You may also complain to the Information Commissioner’s Office. We may update this notice when the service, suppliers or legal requirements change; the date above identifies the current version.
